The Narrative

I am a Computational Chemist and PhD Researcher at Stony Brook University. My research focuses on the evolution of electronic coherence in molecular systems using Ehrenfest dynamics. I've also used ML tools for exploring battery materials and accelerating materials discovery.

With an M.Sc. from IIT Guwahati (CPI 8.88/10) and a B.Sc. B.Ed. from Maharshi Dayanand University, I combine rigorous electronic structure calculations with modern AI tools to advance sustainable energy science.

Beyond research, I serve as a Graduate Teaching Assistant at SBU, an Executive Board Member of the Indian Graduate Student Association, and a member of the American Chemical Society.

Research Portfolio

Click any card to expand the project description.

01 / NONADIABATIC DYNAMICS · Jan 2026 – Present

Electronic Coherence, Decoherence & Recoherence

Supervisor: Prof. Benjamin Levine

Investigates the evolution of electronic coherence in molecular systems using Ehrenfest dynamics with the TAB method to model decoherence and explores recoherence phenomena in nonadiabatic molecular dynamics.

Ehrenfest Dynamics Gaussian Fortran TAB Method
02 / CATALYSIS / GREEN H2 · Nov 2025 – Jan 2026

Oxygen Evolution Reaction on IrO2 and Related Oxides

Supervisor: Prof. Ping Liu

DFT-based catalytic pathway analysis identifying active sites, reaction energetics, and stability trends relevant for improving water-splitting performance — an essential step in green hydrogen production.

DFT VASP Water Splitting
03 / MACHINE LEARNING · Oct 2025 – Nov 2025

Li-Ion Battery Free Energy Predictions via ML

Supervisor: Prof. Sara E. Mason

Developed ML models to evaluate and rank key physical and chemical descriptors for predicting free energy in Li-ion battery systems, supporting faster and more reliable materials screening.

Machine Learning Python Li-Ion Batteries
04 / SPECTROSCOPY · Aug – Oct 2025

XPES Signatures in Salicylideneaniline

Supervisor: Prof. Benjamin Levine

Computational investigation using multireference (CASPT2) and DFT methods to advance accurate computational spectroscopy and validate methodological consistency in predicting core-level ionization energies.

CASPT2 DFT XPES
05 / MASTER'S THESIS · May 2024 – May 2025

Effect of Fluorination on Excited-State Properties in N-Salicylideneaniline

Supervisor: Prof. Aditya N. Panda, IIT Guwahati

Conducted electronic structure calculations to study how fluorination modulates the excited-state properties of N-Salicylideneaniline. The research aims to improve the optoelectronic properties of SA by understanding photophysical and photochemical behaviour under substituent effects.

Electronic Structure Gaussian Excited States Optoelectronics
